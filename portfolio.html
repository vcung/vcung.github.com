Brooklyn: A Case Study of 21st Century Gentrification

This is the final project for Visualization I built with Spencer Meldrum and Justin Shiiba. The assignment was very open: to enhance understanding of any dataset with visualizations. A teammate's recent visit to New York and our mutual interest in social issues inspired us to explore gentrification in Brooklyn, NY. Using census tract data from the U.S Census Bureau, we created interactive choropleth maps from polygon shapefiles with D3.js to show differences in demographics in 2000 and 2010.

Vinobly website

I was the sole Front-End Developer at Vinobly, an early stage wine recommendation startup. I built the overall front-end of the website using HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, and JavaScript from requirements specified in wireframes. I implemented the pages mobile first because the goal is for users to be able to access the web application on their phones when they are browsing at a wine shop. Ultimately, the pages are incorporated into hybrid applications for iOS and Android.

This was my first time working extensively in the front-end (previous classes focused on back-end problems) and collaborating with a UI/UX designer, and it exposed me to the joy and nuances of UX as I continuously made changes based on user testing.
